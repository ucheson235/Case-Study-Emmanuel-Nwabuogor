1. How Raw Data Was Gotten for Summarizing the Number of Customers in Each Group and Suggesting Strategies

  1. Extract Data: I started with a dataset that included:
    • Customer ID: Unique identifier for each customer.
    • Payment Dates: The actual dates payments were made.
    • Expected Payment Dates: The dates payments were supposed to be made.
    • NPS Score: A satisfaction score for each customer (1-10).
  2. Calculate Late Payments: I compared the payment date with the expected date:
    • If the payment date was after the expected date, it was marked as late.
    • For each customer, the number of late payments was counted.
  3. Categorize Customers: Based on their NPS Score and late payments:
    • Satisfied and On-Time: High satisfaction (NPS >= 7) and no late payments.
    • Dissatisfied and On-Time: Low satisfaction (NPS < 7) and no late payments.
    • At Risk: Low satisfaction (NPS < 7) with 1-2 late payments.
    • Critical Risk: Very low satisfaction (NPS <= 5) with more than 2 late payments.
  4. Count Customers in Each Group: The customers were grouped, and the number of customers in each category was calculated:
    • Satisfied and On-Time: 2
    • Dissatisfied and On-Time: 1
    • At Risk: 1
    • Critical Risk: 0
